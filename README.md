A curated list of free and open-source software/apps useful for Applied Biotechnology & Microbiology research.

## Tools List:

### 1. [ImageJ](https://imagej.net/)
- Used for: Image analysis (microscopy, gel electrophoresis)
- Why useful: Quantify bacterial colonies, analyze cell shapes

### 2. [SnapGene Viewer](https://www.snapgene.com/snapgene-viewer/)
- Used for: Plasmid mapping, DNA sequence visualization
- Why useful: Free alternative for molecular biology design

### 3. [RStudio](https://www.rstudio.com/) + [Bioconductor](https://www.bioconductor.org/)
- Used for: Bioinformatics, statistical analysis of biological data
- Why useful: Analyze gene expression, NGS data

### 4. [UCSF ChimeraX](https://www.cgl.ucsf.edu/chimerax/)
- Used for: Molecular visualization, protein structures
- Why useful: Model protein-ligand interactions

### 5. [KBase](https://www.kbase.us/)
- Used for: Systems biology, genome analysis
- Why useful: Predict metabolic pathways in microbes
